It has seen the sun fade from a blazing disk to a star, then to merely the brightest point of light in the sky.


Voyager has no destination. It's not traveling toward something it will reach. Endless motion. Carrying its golden record and its fading instruments deeper into a cosmos that will never be fully explored, never be fully known. It's a journey without end, without purpose except to continue, which is perhaps the greatest purpose of all.

A kind of peace that's difficult to describe — the peace of something that will continue forever, asking nothing of the universe except to pass through it unmolested. Voyager has found this peace, or perhaps always had it, since the day it was launched.
