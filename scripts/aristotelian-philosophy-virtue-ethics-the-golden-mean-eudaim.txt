A bowl's purpose is to hold. A lyre's purpose is to make music. An eye's purpose is to see. Everything that exists has a telos—an end toward which it naturally moves. And for human beings, that telos is eudaimonia. Flourishing. Excellence. You were made for this. Not by design or force. But by nature. Everything within you tends toward this end, whether you realize it or not.


Look at nature. A seed falls. It doesn't question. It simply grows toward what it is meant to become. A tree reaches toward the sky. It doesn't calculate. It fulfills its nature. Everything in the natural world is oriented this way—toward its own completion, its own purpose. And you, too, are part of this. Your nature pulls you toward excellence. Toward the good life. You can resist it. But you can't escape it.


At the center of everything is stillness. The unmoved mover. Eternal. Perfect. Unchanging. Everything moves because of desire for this perfect being. Not because it pulls them with force. But because it's so beautiful, so perfect, that all things are drawn toward it naturally. The stars circle. Life grows. You strive. All because you're pulled toward that perfect stillness at the heart of all things. Toward perfect contemplation.


All your striving toward virtue. All your practice. All your choices. They're not pulling you forward toward something far away. They're drawing you home. Toward the stillness and peace that's always been at your center. Toward the quiet contemplation of what's perfect and eternal. The good life isn't a destination. It's a returning to what you already are, underneath everything else. It's peace. It's home.


Everything in its place. Every creature fulfilling its nature. Every person choosing as they should. The plants growing. The animals moving. The stars turning. And you. Resting in your own excellence. Finding your own mean. Living your own eudaimonia. All of it together. All of it in harmony. Moving because of love for what's eternal and perfect. Moving toward home. Toward stillness. Toward peace.


And so he rests. The man who walked the pathways. Who asked questions. Who observed. Who taught. Who lived the golden mean. Who chose well. Who loved wisely. Who became who he was meant to be. His work is done. His nature is fulfilled. In the stillness of night, in the quiet of having lived well, he sleeps. And you sleep too. Home. At last. At peace.
