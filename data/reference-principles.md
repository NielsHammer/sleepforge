# SleepForge Reference Principles
*Extracted from 15 high-performing sleep/philosophy YouTube videos, May 2026*

---

## Thumbnail Patterns

### 1. Icon Grid (HIGHEST CTR — 200K–500K views)
Black background, star field, all-caps bold white header text, **red underline swoosh arrow**, two rows of labelled concept cards.

- Text fills top 25% of frame — ultra-bold, near edge-to-edge
- Red swoosh underline is the #1 visual anchor
- 10–12 icon cards in 2 rows, slightly cropped at edges ("there's more")
- Monochrome icons with concept name below each
- **Best titles:** "N Most Important X", "All of X Explained", "N [Category] Ideas"

### 2. Dramatic Two-Tone
Pure black bg, single vivid 2-color illustration (teal + red), oversized text top-left.
- **Best titles:** Single concept ("Brain F*ck", "Consciousness")

### 3. Dark Atmospheric (closest to our aesthetic)
Star field + white outline figure + glowing red text. Mysterious, existential.
- **Best titles:** "The Most [Adjective] Philosophy to Fall Asleep to"

### 4. Portrait Grid
Philosopher portraits in cards, header at top with red swoosh. Academic/authority.
- **Best titles:** "All of X", "N Philosophers Explained"

## Script Patterns

### Encyclopedic Flow (best for 1-hour videos)
- Hook: 1 brief framing sentence ("In this session, we explore…")
- Body: 25–30 concepts × 200–250 words each ≈ 60 minutes
- Pacing: present tense, continuous flow, no CTAs in narration
- Style: medium vocabulary, no jargon, invites the mind to drift

### Single Concept Deep Dive
- Hook: question or paradox
- 600–700 words per concept, long winding sentences
- Soft philosophical voice, never preachy
