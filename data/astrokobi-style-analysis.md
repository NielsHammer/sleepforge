## TITLE PATTERNS (Top 10)

### 1. "What If [extreme space scenario]?"
Highest curiosity gap. Works by forcing viewer to imagine something impossible.
- "What if a Needle hit Earth at light speed?"
- "What If Voyager 1 Turned On It's Camera?"
- "What if the Moon hit Earth?"
- "What if the Moon turned into a Black Hole?"

### 2. "[N] [Things] [Superlative Quality]"
List format with strong superlative. Always specific numbers.
- "4 Planets Better For Life Than Earth"
- "15 Insane Events You Will Miss"
- "The 5 Most Insane Planets In The Universe"
- "4 Most Terrifying Planets in The Universe"
- "4 REAL Events You and I will miss"

### 3. "How [Are We Still / Could We] [doing X]?"
Wonder and disbelief framing. Makes the known seem impossible.
- "How Are We Still In Contact With Voyager?"
- "How will The Universe end?"

### 4. "This [Specific Cosmic Object] [Shocking Statement]"
Discovery + reaction. Specific noun (not vague "a planet").
- "This Terrifying Planet is made of Pure DIAMOND"
- "This Black Hole has SHOCKED Scientists"
- "This Mars Rover is Dying"
- "This Star Is About To Explode!"

### 5. "What Came Before [X]?" / "What Is Outside [X]?"
Deep mystery framing. Makes viewer question fundamental assumptions.
- "What Came Before The Big Bang?"
- "Solving The Hardest Problem In Physics" (the answer is about what's outside the universe)

### 6. "Solving The [Hardest/Biggest] [Problem]"
Challenge + payoff framing. Implies that the video delivers a resolution.
- "Solving The Hardest Problem In Physics"

### 7. "You Will [NEVER / Always] [miss/see] [X]"
Personal stakes. Makes it about the viewer's lifetime.
- "You will NEVER see The Milky Way again"
- "15 Insane Events You Will Miss"

### 8. "Scientists [Just Discovered / Are Terrified By / Are REALLY Close To] [X]"
Authority + urgency. Feels like breaking news.
- "Scientists Are REALLY Close To Finding Planet 9"
- "NASA Probably Just Found Life On Mars"
- "Scientists Received a Message from ALIENS"

### 9. "[Institution] Just [Verb] [Object]"
Similar to above — event-driven news hook.
- "Voyager 1 is FINALLY responding again!"
- "NASA Just Found Two Super Earths that can Support Life"
- "JWST Finally Discovers Black Hole Stars?!"

### 10. "Could [Event] [Happen Again / Happen To Us]?"
Concern + existential stakes.
- "Could The Big Bang Happen Again?"

## THUMBNAIL PATTERNS

### Typography
- **Font:** Bold, condensed, sans-serif — Impact-style or Bebas Neue-style
- **Size:** ENORMOUS. Hook text fills 30-50% of the thumbnail height
- **Color:** White or near-white (#FFFFFF, #F5F5F5)
- **Effect:** Subtle drop shadow or outline for legibility
- **Case:** ALL CAPS always
- **Spacing:** Tight — condensed font with minimal letter-spacing

### Hook Text (overlay text)
2-4 words maximum. Always a REACTION or STATEMENT, never a topic description.

| Hook | Type | Used when |
|------|------|-----------|
| "THIS IS BAD" | alarm reaction | threatening discovery |
| "SHOULD NOT BE POSSIBLE" | impossibility | something defying physics |
| "THEY DID" | shocked confirmation | someone actually tried something extreme |
| "YES." | plain confirmation | answer to a seemingly impossible question |
| "THEY LIED" | betrayal/reveal | institutions were wrong |
| "EARTH 2.0" | label | comparison to an Earth-like planet |
| "WAIT" | reconsideration | challenges assumptions |
| "WHAT IS OUTSIDE THE UNIVERSE?" | mystery question | when the answer IS the hook |
| "NEVER" | absolute | things that can't happen |
| "IMPOSSIBLE" | impossibility | defies known physics |

### Image Composition
- **Primary subject:** ONE clear focal object — a planet, spacecraft, nebula, star, or structural element
- **Scale comparison:** Often two objects side by side (small planet vs huge star)
- **Backdrop:** Deep dark space — black or very dark blue
- **Color temperature:** Cool (space blues/purples) + one warm highlight (sun, explosion, planet glow)
- **Style:** PHOTOREALISTIC — not illustrated, not stylized
- **NO:** Borders, gold frames, chalk drawings, sketches, ornate overlays

### What They NEVER Do
- No "sleep", "drift off", "meditation" in titles or descriptions
- No serif fonts
- No muted/dark text
- No text below ~60px at final 1280x720
- No cluttered compositions with multiple text elements
- No question marks on the hook text (the hook is a statement, the title is the question)

## SUBJECT/TOPIC PATTERNS

### High-performing categories:
1. **Scale comparisons** — planets vs stars vs universe size
2. **Future events** — events humans will or won't live to see
3. **Missions and probes** — Voyager, Hubble, JWST, Cassini, Artemis
4. **Black holes** — formation, event horizon, spaghettification
5. **Earth threats** — asteroids, solar events, galactic collisions
6. **Exoplanets** — habitable zones, Earth-like worlds, extreme planets
7. **End-of-universe scenarios** — heat death, Big Rip, Andromeda collision
8. **Physics puzzles** — speed of light, time dilation, quantum mechanics
9. **Ancient mysteries** — Big Bang, dark matter, what preceded time

### Topics that underperform:
- Pure theory without visual stakes
- Historical space history without a hook angle
- Mission updates without drama (unless something surprising happened)

## APPLICATION TO SLEEPLESS ASTRONOMER

When generating thumbnails and titles for Sleepless Astronomer:
1. Titles use AstroKobi patterns — NO sleep keywords
2. Hooks are reactions/statements in ALL CAPS (2-4 words)
3. Font: bold white sans-serif (Bebas Neue, Impact, or Barlow Condensed ExtraBold)
4. Images: photoreal space imagery from space-library-v1
5. Composition: one clear focal subject + dark space background
6. No period-authentic constraint (this is not a philosophy/history channel)
7. Drop shadows on text for legibility over space backgrounds
8. Bright saturated colors — not muted or desaturated
