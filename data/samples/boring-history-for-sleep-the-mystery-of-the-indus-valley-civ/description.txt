The Indus Valley Civilization flourished thousands of years ago in what is now Pakistan and India, leaving behind meticulously planned cities and countless mysteries that historians continue to ponder. In this hour-long exploration, we'll wander through the ancient streets of Mohenjo-daro and Harappa, unraveling the quiet story of a remarkably peaceful Bronze Age culture.

What's in this video:
• A contemplative tour through the archaeological remains of Mohenjo-daro, one of the world's earliest planned cities with advanced drainage systems and orderly streets
• The fascinating history of Harappa and what artifacts reveal about daily life, trade, and social structure in the Indus Valley
• Theories about the civilization's mysterious decline and the still-undeciphered Indus script that guards its secrets
• Meditative reflections on a culture that valued order, cleanliness, and harmony over conquest and warfare
