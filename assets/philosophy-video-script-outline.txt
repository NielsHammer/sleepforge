1. Socrates and the hemlock - Socrates chooses death over dishonoring his principles, teaching students in a stone prison courtyard that truth is worth any cost.

2. Plato and the cave - Plato shows a prisoner the light outside the cave, a turning point for understanding the difference between appearance and reality.

3. Aristotle classifies life - Aristotle arranges stone animal figures in his academy, demonstrating the first systematic attempt to categorize nature and reason.

4. Marcus Aurelius writing - Marcus Aurelius sits on a street bench writing his journal, making private notes about stoic self-discipline and inner freedom.

5. Nietzsche and eternal recurrence - Nietzsche walks a curved stone path past ruins at dusk, reflecting on whether he would gladly relive this life forever.

6. Descartes doubts - Descartes sits alone in a candlelit study, turning over a skull as he questions the certainty of the material world.

7. Kant and the moral law - Kant walks under lantern light in Königsberg, thinking about duty and the universal principles that should govern action.

8. Locke on experience - Locke writes by a window with apple and key nearby, illustrating that mind and knowledge begin from experience, not from innate ideas.

9. Hume and the senses - Hume sits at a tavern table, watching a river of thought flow past as he considers how impressions form the basis of belief.

10. Mill and the greatest happiness - Mill sits in a garden as bees hover, thinking about how actions should be judged by the happiness they promote for the many.

11. Confucius teaching virtue - Confucius sits beneath a pine tree instructing his students on the importance of ritual, family, and proper conduct.

12. Buddha under the fig tree - The Buddha meditates beneath the fig tree, embodying the quiet path beyond desire and suffering.

13. Ship of Theseus - A wooden ship at a moonlit harbor is rebuilt plank by plank, asking whether the same object survives change.

14. Mind and body split - A stone statue split in two shows a physical body on one side and a thought-filled interior on the other.

15. Free will crossroads - A lone traveler stands at a fork in a dark stone road, contemplating whether actions are chosen freely or predetermined.

16. Ethics of action - A scale balances a stone heart against a stone sword, symbolizing the tension between compassion and duty.

17. Pragmatism in practice - A builder lays stone steps by hand, showing philosophy tested through real outcomes rather than abstract doctrine.

18. Phenomenology of perception - A figure touches a floating stone shape, emphasizing experience over explanation.

19. Existential choice - A lone thinker stands on a cliff edge, feeling the weight of self-created meaning in an indifferent landscape.

20. Nihilism emptiness - A dark chamber with a single empty chair captures the unsettling openness of a world without inherent meaning.

21. Sartre in the cafe - Sartre writes in a rain-speckled cafe window, thinking about freedom, bad faith, and human choice.

22. Aquinas in the monastery - Aquinas works in a dim scriptorium, synthesizing reason and faith in quiet stone corridors.

23. Philosophy of life - A long stone path leads through changing seasons, symbolizing the search for a meaningful life.

24. Meaning of life horizon - A traveler reaches a bright horizon after a long dark journey, asking whether purpose is on the other side or generated along the way.
